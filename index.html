ABOUT US

We are creators from a background in visual and digital media, focusing on the relationship between technology and human perception. We attempt to observe and understand emotions not through language, but through bodily movement. This project is based on AI motion recognition, translating human gestures into emotional signals and exploring whether emotions can truly be “recognized.” By training and classifying three fundamental emotional states—Calm, Anxiety, and Happiness—we developed a system where users interact without words, relying solely on physical expression. Using accessible machine learning tools, we transform complex AI processes into an intuitive and perceptible experience. In an era where artificial intelligence increasingly participates in interpretation and decision-making, we hope this project prompts reflection: are emotions truly understood, or merely identified and defined?

PROJECT

In today’s digital environment, artificial intelligence is increasingly involved in analyzing and interpreting human behavior and emotions. However, most emotion recognition systems rely heavily on facial expressions or voice input, which to some extent limits the diversity of emotional expression. Therefore, this project approaches the problem from a different perspective—using body movement as the primary basis for recognition, with facial expression as a secondary reference, to explore a more holistic way of perceiving human emotions.

This project is developed using Google’s Teachable Machine platform. By collecting and training motion data, we built a model capable of recognizing three fundamental emotional states: Calm, Anxiety, and Happiness. In the interactive experience, users do not need to provide verbal input; instead, they express emotions through specific body movements, which the system recognizes and responds to in real time, creating an intuitive human–computer interaction process.

GUIDE

Perform the following gestures to interact with the neural model.

CALM

Stand still. Arms relaxed at sides. Minimal spatial interference.

ANXIETY

Closed posture. Hands near face. Rapid inward movements.

HAPPINESS

Expansive movements. Arms raised. Open chest posture.
