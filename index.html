ABOUT US

We are creators from a background in visual and digital media, focusing on the relationship between technology and human perception. We attempt to observe and understand emotions not through language, but through bodily movement. This project is based on AI motion recognition, translating human gestures into emotional signals and exploring whether emotions can truly be “recognized.”

By training and classifying three fundamental emotional states—Calm, Anxiety, and Happiness—we developed a system where users interact without words, relying solely on physical expression. Using accessible machine learning tools, we transform complex AI processes into an intuitive and perceptible experience. In an era where artificial intelligence increasingly participates in interpretation and decision-making, we hope this project prompts reflection: are emotions truly understood, or merely identified and defined?

PROJECT

In today’s digital environment, artificial intelligence is increasingly involved in analyzing and interpreting human behavior and emotions. However, most emotion recognition systems rely heavily on facial expressions or voice input, which to some extent limits the diversity of emotional expression. Therefore, this project approaches the problem from a different perspective—using body movement as the primary basis for recognition, with facial expression as a secondary reference, to explore a more holistic way of perceiving human emotions.

This project is developed using Google’s Teachable Machine platform. By collecting and training motion data, we built a model capable of recognizing three fundamental emotional states: Calm, Anxiety, and Happiness. In the interactive experience, users do not need to provide verbal input; instead, they express emotions through specific body movements, which the system recognizes and responds to in real time, creating an intuitive human–computer interaction process.

Through this approach, we aim to move beyond traditional face-centered emotion recognition and shift attention toward the expressive potential of the human body. At the same time, by leveraging accessible machine learning tools, we transform complex technical processes into participatory and experiential interactions, allowing users to understand the technology through engagement and reconsider how emotions are perceived and defined.

CALIBRATION GUIDE

CALM

Maintain biological homeostasis. Neutral spine, hands relaxed at sides or on lap. Minimal kinetic output.

ANXIETY

Kinetic overload. Bring hands towards face, hunch shoulders, or execute small, restless repetitive movements.

HAPPINESS

Spatial expansion. Raise arms high, expand chest, or perform open, energetic gestures. High energy output.
